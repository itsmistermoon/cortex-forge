# Cortex Forge — Visual Identity

## Purpose

The visual identity of Cortex Forge exists to reinforce the project's central metaphor:

> Transforming raw information into structured knowledge.

Artwork should support the product and its communication. It is not intended to build an extensive fictional universe. Every image should communicate one clear product message.

**Canonical tagline:** *Forge Knowledge. Empower Agents.*

Use this tagline when the image requires a supporting line. Do not substitute aspirational or generic alternatives.

---

## Reference Image

The GitHub Hero (`cortex-forge-promo.png`) is the canonical reference for the visual identity. It establishes the tone, palette, character, and atmosphere that all future artwork should build from.

When instructions in this document conflict with what the reference image does, treat the image as the authoritative source for visual decisions and this document as the guide for artistic intent.

---

# Visual Premise

Technology has advanced so far that it is visually indistinguishable from arcane magic.

Magic is never supernatural. It is the visual representation of information, memory, reasoning and knowledge. The result should feel like ancient wisdom powered by futuristic technology.

---

# Core Visual Elements

## Brigid

The visual embodiment of wisdom and craftsmanship. Inspired by the Celtic goddess Brigid, she operates the Forge and represents the intelligence behind Cortex Forge. She is calm, confident and approachable.

Brigid has a physical, directed relationship with the Forge. She works with it — not against an enemy. Her posture is focused and deliberate, never aggressive or combative. She should feel closer to an architect, researcher or master artisan than a fighter.

Her expression ranges from calm concentration to quiet satisfaction. She never shows tension, urgency or aggression.

Brigid is the face of Cortex Forge, but she does not need to appear in every illustration.

---

## The Forge

The symbolic heart of Cortex Forge. The Forge represents the transformation of information into structured knowledge. It is not a medieval blacksmith workshop. It is an ancient arcane machine that processes knowledge through magical technology.

---

## The Hammer

The Hammer is Brigid's primary tool. It is a precision instrument of transformation, not a weapon of force. When she strikes, she shapes — the gesture is controlled and purposeful. The hammer channels energy into the Forge; it does not destroy.

---

## What Lies on the Anvil

The object being transformed on the Forge represents raw knowledge becoming structured. In the GitHub Hero this takes the form of a luminous brain — an acceptable starting point for that image only.

For all future images, prefer a **node lattice**: a network of interconnected points with flowing lines of energy between them, rendered in the Three Fires palette. The lattice should feel three-dimensional and branching in multiple directions — a graph, not a tree. This communicates interconnected knowledge more precisely than a vertical or linear structure. Avoid anatomical or biological forms. Knowledge should feel architectural, not organic.

---

## The Trisquetra

The primary symbol of Cortex Forge. It represents the continuous refinement cycle of knowledge and the harmony of the Three Fires. It may appear engraved, forged, projected as holograms or constructed from pure energy.

The Trisquetra should feel integrated into the composition — part of the environment, the forge, or the lattice. Avoid floating it in isolation as a logo element; when it appears disconnected from the scene it reads as an afterthought.

---

## The Three Fires

Each fire has its own personality.

### Wisdom Fire

Purple

Represents:

* Insight
* Planning
* Understanding

Its movement is elegant and fluid. It reveals.

---

### Transformation Fire

Amber

Represents:

* Creation
* Synthesis
* Refinement

It is energetic and explosive. It builds.

---

### Regeneration Fire

Teal

Represents:

* Memory
* Maintenance
* Restoration

It moves slowly and organically. It restores.

The fires have no fixed position.

# Artistic Direction

Approximate visual balance:

* 60% Celtic Arcane Fantasy
* 30% Ghost in the Shell
* 10% Premium AI Product Marketing

The 30% Ghost in the Shell is not decorative — it is what distinguishes the visual language from generic fantasy. It should be felt in: holographic overlays of sacred geometry, data structures visible as light, interfaces that look like they process information rather than decorate space, and a sense that the arcane elements are running on something.

Lighting is cinematic and dramatic.

Technology is represented as magical holographic interfaces made from:

* sacred geometry
* constellations
* neural networks
* flowing light
* energetic patterns

Technology should feel spiritual rather than mechanical.

Knowledge should have visible form.

# Composition

## GitHub Hero

Epic, dense and timeless. The reference image is the model: rich environment, Brigid as central figure, the Three Fires present, the Trisquetra recurring as a motif. This image type has license to fill the frame.

## All Other Image Types

The artwork should resemble premium product marketing rather than fantasy illustration.

Every image must communicate a single idea. Visual hierarchy is more important than visual complexity.

Prefer:

* one hero subject
* dark background with generous negative space
* large readable titles
* two or three supporting visual elements maximum

The background should be dark and largely empty. Supporting elements carry meaning — decorative elements that exist only to fill space are prohibited.

The image should remain readable even as a thumbnail.

---

# Marketing Principle

The artwork exists to communicate one product message.

Do not attempt to visualize every feature simultaneously.

Show concepts.

Documentation explains details.

---

# Image Types

## GitHub Hero

Introduce Cortex Forge.

Epic and timeless. Rich composition is appropriate here.

---

## Release Announcement

Communicate the release itself.

Highlight only two or three major changes.

The version number should dominate.

Layout: Brigid occupies the center or one side of the frame; release text and feature callouts occupy the opposite side. The illustration and the text panel are two distinct zones — do not mix them. Feature callouts are minimal: name and one short line each.

---

## Feature Spotlight

Focus on a single capability.

The feature is the hero.

Do not embed the image type name ("Feature Spotlight") as text inside the image. Type labels are added in context — in a README, a blog post, a release page — never hardcoded into the artwork itself.

---

## Documentation Header

Represent one technical concept.

Minimal composition.

---

## Architecture Diagram

Readable first.

Beautiful second.

Visual clarity always wins.

---

# Never Show

* swords
* blood
* gore
* demons
* dragons
* Nordic runes
* Christian iconography
* military aesthetics
* post-apocalyptic environments
* steampunk

Avoid generic fantasy magic.

Avoid conventional cyberpunk interfaces.

Avoid code floating in the air.

Avoid UI that resembles operating systems or terminals.

Knowledge should be represented through geometry, light, constellations and holographic structures.

---

# Design Principles

Every image should feel:

* Intelligent
* Premium
* Timeless
* Purposeful
* Cinematic

Knowledge before power.

Structure before spectacle.

Clarity before decoration.
